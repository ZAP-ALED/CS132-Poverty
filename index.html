Self-Rated Poverty in the Philippines

Self-rated poverty is an essential measure of economic well-being, reflecting individuals' perceptions of their financial situation. This study explores trends, demographic patterns, and regional disparities in self-rated poverty from 2001 to 2024.

Research Questions

  • What are the trends in self-rated poverty in the Philippines from 2001 to 2024?
  • Which regions or demographic groups consistently report higher self-rated poverty levels?
  • Does self-rated poverty differ across urban and rural areas in the Philippines?
  • How do self-rated poverty trends compare before, during, and after economic crises?

Data Source

Our research utilizes data from Social Weather Stations (SWS). The dataset can be accessed at: Social Weather Stations, 2025.

Methodology

Our study employs statistical analysis and data visualization to examine trends and disparities in self-rated poverty. We compare regional and demographic variations and analyze the impact of economic crises.
